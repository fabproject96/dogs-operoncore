But is a monthly subscription worth it compared to a one-time course? Here's an honest breakdown.

Who Is Doggy Dan?

Dan Abdelnoor — known professionally as Doggy Dan — is a New Zealand-based professional dog trainer and behaviorist. He has been featured on TV programs and is known for rehabilitating severely troubled dogs. His philosophy centers on what he calls the "dog listener" approach: before you can train commands, your dog needs to see you as the calm, confident decision-maker in the household. It's a leadership model, not a dominance model — and the distinction matters.

Unlike some online trainers whose credentials are thin, Dan has documented case studies and video evidence of transformations across hundreds of dogs, from reactive rescues to severe separation anxiety cases.

The Separation Anxiety Module — Standout Feature

If your dog struggles with being left alone, this is where Doggy Dan's membership pulls ahead of most alternatives. The dedicated separation anxiety module is one of the most thorough available online. Dan walks through:

  • Diagnosing true separation anxiety vs. boredom or under-exercise
  • The "calm departure" protocol — how you leave matters as much as how long you're gone
  • Desensitizing departure triggers step by step (keys, shoes, bag)
  • Building alone-time tolerance from 5 seconds upward without triggering panic
  • How to handle night anxiety and crate stress

Many owners report measurable improvement within 2–3 weeks when following the protocol consistently. This single module alone is worth the monthly fee for owners dealing with destructive or howling dogs.

What Works Well

  • 250+ videos covering every real-world scenario — very unlikely you won't find your specific problem
  • The calm leadership framework reduces anxiety-driven behaviors at the root, not just the symptom
  • Live Q&A gives access to Dan directly — rare for this price point
  • The private community is active and moderated
  • Works for puppies and adult dogs — the methodology scales
  • Recurring commissions for affiliates signal a product people keep paying for — churn is low

What's Less Ideal

  • Monthly subscription model — cost adds up if you keep it long-term (though most problems solve in 1–3 months)
  • The "5 Golden Rules" framework is Dan's proprietary model — some professional trainers debate the leadership terminology, though the practical results are solid
  • Video library is large enough to feel overwhelming — no rigid step-by-step progression like Brain Training for Dogs
  • No offline/downloadable content — requires internet access

Who It's Best For

  • Owners dealing with anxiety-driven behaviors: separation anxiety, reactivity, excessive barking
  • Multi-dog households where pack dynamics are causing issues
  • Owners who want live access to a trainer, not just a pre-recorded course
  • Rescue dog owners working through behavioral rehabilitation
  • Anyone who has tried basic obedience training but still has a stressed or unruly dog
